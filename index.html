A topic related to social good that I'm passionate about is the right to housing. I've only been renting for a few years now but the market and subsequent access to housing in the US over the past decade has changed immensely and in the wrong direction. It's a complicated issue, as the solution unfortuntely isn't simply to build more houses.

One aspect is the gentrification of urban areas and the displacement of long-time residents of the community that often occurs as a result. Not only is this a disservice to those who have created the very culture that is now valued in these areas, but it also makes the city as a whole more exclusive to the wealthy. Building new affordable housing along with restaurants, retail, and other desireable features is essential to allow for the rejuvenation of low-income areas while maintaining most of the current population and upholding access to safe housing.

Another part of the issue is the commodification of housing by both large property management companies as well as individual homeowners. Landlords do not provide any kind of beneficial service and do not contribute to the general wellbeing or betterment of society. The hoarding of a profile of rental properties is a massive ethical violation that only perpetuates the poor maintenance of homes and thus lower standards of living. This also feeds into the widening wealth gap in the US that keeps money in the hands of the rich and basic necessities out of the hands of everyone else.

I don't claim to have all the answers to solving this issue, but I believe that housing should be limited to one per person/household and largely subsidized by the government so that individuals are only paying for the maintenance and recurring utility costs to live in the home. Homelessness is an issue we choose to have by prioritizing capitalist interests over human rights.
